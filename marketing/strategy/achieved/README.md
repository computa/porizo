# Achieved Strategy

This folder records strategies we actually tried or completed. "Achieved" does not mean "successful"; it means the strategy was executed enough to produce learning.

Each entry should answer:

- What was the strategy?
- What did we try?
- What happened?
- What did we learn?
- What should we avoid repeating?

## Ledger

### 2026-04-28 Consumer App GTM

File: [`2026-04-28-consumer-app-gtm.md`](2026-04-28-consumer-app-gtm.md)

What we tried: a layered consumer-app GTM plan around product activation, L2 content/UGC, ASO/SEO, and paid amplification.

What happened: execution fragmented into many channel plans and content/tooling work before there was enough public proof or channel-message fit. Paid traffic and email did not produce meaningful user growth.

Learning: the sequencing diagnosis was directionally right, but the operating mode still became too scattered. Future strategy needs one live push, one proof asset goal, and fewer simultaneous channels.

### Traffic Strategy / SEO-GEO Scaffolding

File: [`traffic-strategy.md`](traffic-strategy.md)

What we tried: broad traffic and content scaffolding across search, SEO/GEO, and seasonal gift intent.

What happened: useful as research, but too broad for the immediate distribution bottleneck. Search Console showed almost no non-brand web demand, so SEO is not yet the primary acquisition engine.

Learning: keep SEO/blog as a proof and landing layer, not the main short-term growth engine.
