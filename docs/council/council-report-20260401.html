LLM Council Report

Porizo iOS App Redesign Review — April 1, 2026 — 5 Advisors, 5 Peer Reviewers, 1 Chairman

Question: Should these redesign specs (design-like-a-yc-startup-spec.md + comprehensive.md) drive implementation for the Porizo iOS app? The app has low downloads, low registration, and low testing. The redesign targets women in emotionally expressive gifting.

The Verdict

These documents should NOT get V2 versions in their current form. They are 1,500+ lines of interior design for a building with a foot-traffic problem.

Instead, produce three much shorter documents:

Document 1: "The Monday Ship" (1 page)
Implementation-ready. Remove StoryElementsCard + SongProgressIndicator + ChatHeader dedup. Skip voice step for first-timers. Ship to TestFlight Monday.
Document 2: "The Test Protocol" (1 page)
5 guerrilla test scripts. Include Test 0: show App Store listing to strangers. Decision gates determine next phase.
Document 3: "The Direction" (3-5 pages)
Keep 4-moment framework. Add recipient-as-growth-engine, occasion reactivation, "90 seconds" as headline. Kill all debate/review sections.

The One Thing to Do First

Show the current App Store listing to 5 strangers and ask "what does this app do?"

Zero dollars. Zero engineering. One afternoon. If they can't answer, the problem is positioning, not design tokens.

Advisor Alignment

Where All 5 Agree

  • CONSENSUS Ship the 3 obvious subtractions immediately (scaffolding removal + voice step skip)
  • CONSENSUS Run guerrilla tests before any major implementation
  • CONSENSUS "90 seconds" is the strongest marketing asset and is being underused

  • CLASH Expansionist vs Everyone: Expansionist sees a viral gifting protocol with 5-10x LTV. 4/5 peer reviewers say you can't build growth loops on an unvalidated product.
  • CLASH Contrarian vs Executor: Contrarian says "don't produce V2 docs, let data decide." Executor says "ship subtractions Monday, test next week, THEN decide."

  • MISSED Competitive landscape: What do people do instead? Does this market exist?
  • MISSED Pricing: What does this cost the user? When does money come up?
  • MISSED Solo developer constraint: All advice assumes team capacity that doesn't exist
  • MISSED Voice enrollment in v1: Should it exist at all before PMF?

Advisor Responses

  The Contrarian +

"These documents are a redesign looking for a diagnosis."

Zero data on where users drop off. The palette debate consumes disproportionate document space with no user interviews backing it. "90 seconds" is stated as a promise but never validated against production latency. Recipient experience is acknowledged as critical then deferred to Phase 2-3. The spec has 1,025 lines of design philosophy and zero lines of observed user behavior.

Recommendation: Produce a 1-page test protocol, run 5 sessions, let the data write the spec.

  The First Principles Thinker +

"Redesigning the interior of a restaurant nobody walks into."

95% of word count is post-registration UX. The fundamental question (why are downloads low?) is never asked. No mention of App Store screenshots, preview video, keyword strategy. The four-moment framework is internal engineering narrative, not user need. The documents are "sophisticated planning that delays contact with reality."

Recommendation: Fix App Store listing (day one, zero engineering). Instrument the funnel. Strip first-run to minimum. Ship testable builds weekly.

  The Expansionist +

"Porizo is not a song app. It is a viral gifting protocol."

Three missed opportunities: (1) The recipient IS the growth engine, not a downstream extension. (2) "90 seconds" is the entire go-to-market, not a UI label. (3) Occasion-triggered reactivation is completely absent. The redesign is solving "make the funnel less leaky" when the opportunity is "make every creation seed two more."

Peer review note: 4/5 reviewers flagged this as the biggest blind spot. Growth strategy requires validated product-market fit first.

  The Outsider +

"After 1,500 lines, I still don't know what a personalized song sounds like."

No before/after example of user input vs. song output. Voice enrollment mentioned but never explained. "90 seconds" promise but 5% of users wait longer. Pricing treated as backend concern, not product design. What would make me download: a 10-second video of someone RECEIVING a song. What would make me leave: "Sign in with Apple" with no idea what I get.

"The documents are talking to themselves."

  The Executor +

Critical path: Ship Monday, test by Friday.

Three things ship this week: (1) Remove StoryElementsCardView (30 min). (2) Remove SongProgressIndicator (30 min). (3) Skip voice step for first-timers (2 hours). Everything else is blocked by guerrilla tests that only the developer can run. Phases 1-3 are "strategic docs, not implementation specs."

Recommendation: Ship 3 subtractions Monday → TestFlight → 5 people → Results determine Phase 1.

Peer Review Highlights (5 reviewers) +

Consensus strongest: First Principles Thinker (4/5 votes). "The only response that correctly prioritizes discovery before solution."

Consensus blind spot: Expansionist (4/5 votes). "Builds growth strategy on unvalidated product. Viral loops require a product people want to share; that has not been proven."

Gaps all five missed:
