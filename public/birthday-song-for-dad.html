FAQ

Birthday Song for Dad questions.

Will Dad actually like a song?

Most "Dad doesn't want anything" dads keep the song. The 60-second length is short enough not to feel like an obligation, and the specificity (his nickname, his joke, his truck) makes it land harder than any tie.

Will the song actually feel personal?

Yes — the lyrics come from the specific memory you share about him, so it's unmistakably about him, not a generic Dad song. You choose the musical style and an AI vocalist performs it. A child gifting a father one specific, true detail is one of the highest-impact ways to use it.

What kind of memory works best?

Concrete over abstract. "He always made the same joke about the lawnmower" beats "he's funny." A nickname, a phrase, a thing he always did. Porizo expands the detail into full lyrics.

Can I make it a country song? A rap? A folk song?

Yes — choose the genre when you create the song. Pop, country, R&B, Afropop, folk, acoustic, hip-hop, indie. Pick the sound that fits Dad.

How long does it take?

Preview in under 90 seconds; full song in about three minutes. You can re-roll lyrics, beat, vocal, or one section until it lands.

Can the song reference a tough relationship honestly?

Yes. You give Porizo the detail and the tone you want. The lyric does not have to be saccharine — songs work for complicated relationships too.
