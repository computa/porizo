FAQ

Grandma birthday song questions.

How does my grandma play this? Does she need an app?

No. The song is a web link. Open it on your phone, hand it to her, hit play. It plays in the browser. If she's used FaceTime once, she'll handle this fine.

Can I make it sound like me singing?

Yes — voice cloning is included on Plus and Pro. After enrolling your voice (6–10 short phrases, once), Porizo sings every song in your own voice. For a grandma birthday, hearing your voice is the part that hits.

What if multiple grandkids want to give the song together?

Use the enrolled voice of one grandkid. Then mention all the grandkids' names in the memory you describe — the lyric will weave them in. Or enroll each grandkid separately and make a different song from each.

How long is the song? Can I make it longer for a 90th?

Songs are 45–90 seconds. For a milestone, that length is right — long enough to land, short enough that she plays it three times in a row.

Can I send it before the party so she hears it alone first?

Yes — recommended. Most grandmas appreciate hearing it privately before any public moment. Then play it at the party if the moment fits.

How does this compare to Songfinch for a grandma birthday?

Songfinch uses human composers and ships in 4–7 days for $179.99+. Porizo ships in minutes for $9.99/month and adds voice cloning. For a grandma birthday where the moment matters now, the speed and the voice recognition matter.
