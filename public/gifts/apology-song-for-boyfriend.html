FAQ

Apology song questions.

Isn't an apology song kind of dramatic?

Only if the song is dramatic. Porizo lets you pick the genre and tone — an acoustic, quiet, 60-second song often lands much better than a long text. The brevity is part of why it works.

What if he thinks I'm trying to skip the real conversation?

The song should not replace the real conversation. It works best as the prelude — something he can hear before the in-person talk, so he knows you've thought about it. Then have the conversation. The song earns you the conversation.

Will the song be in my voice?

Yes — voice cloning is on Plus and Pro. Hearing your own voice say the apology is what makes it land. A stranger singing your apology defeats the purpose.

What should I include in the lyric?

Three things: what specifically happened, what you would do differently, and the part you are owning. Avoid generalizations like 'sorry I've been off lately' — name the actual moment.

How long should an apology song be?

Short. 60–75 seconds. Long apology songs sound performative. Short ones sound real.

What if he doesn't accept it?

Then the song wasn't the problem; the situation needs more than music. A song apology is the right format only when both of you are already on the way back to each other.
