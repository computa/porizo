FAQ

Grandpa birthday song questions.

How does my grandpa play the song? Does he need an app?

No. The song is a web link. Open it on your phone, hand it to him, hit play. It plays in the browser. If he's used a voicemail this decade, he'll be fine.

Can I make it sound like me singing?

Yes — voice cloning is included on Plus and Pro. Record 6–10 short phrases once and Porizo will sing every song in your own voice.

What if my grandfather isn't a music guy?

Most grandfathers who 'don't really listen to music' keep these songs anyway. The reason is the specificity — it's about him, not about music.

Can multiple grandkids give the song together?

Use one enrolled voice and mention all the grandkids' names in the memory. Or enroll each grandkid separately and make a different version of the same song.

Can I include the great-grandkids in the lyric?

Yes. Include them in the memory you describe. The lyric will weave the names across generations.

What if I want to give the song privately, not at a party?

Most birthdays end up that way. Send it as a text the morning of his birthday. Let him hear it alone first. The reaction you get back is usually quieter than a party and twice as real.
